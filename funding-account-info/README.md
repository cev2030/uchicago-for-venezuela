# Donate — UChicago For Venezuela

All donations support earthquake relief for Venezuelan families and student
volunteers: buying medical supplies, food, rescue equipment, and logistics
(see [`../supplies-needed/`](../supplies-needed/) for the current needs list).

Donations are received by **1001 Ideas, INC**, a U.S.-based 501(c)(3)
nonprofit organization. **Donations from U.S. taxpayers are tax-deductible to
the extent allowed by law.** (See [Tax-Deductibility](#tax-deductibility--501c3-information) below.)

arrive almost instantly. Most U.S. banking apps have Zelle built in.

## Tax-Deductibility — 501(c)(3) information

**1001 Ideas, INC** is organized as a **501(c)(3) tax-exempt nonprofit** under
the U.S. Internal Revenue Code.

### What this means for you as a donor

- If you are a U.S. taxpayer, your donation is **tax-deductible to the extent
  allowed by law**, provided you **itemize deductions** on your federal income
  tax return (Schedule A of Form 1040). Donors who take the standard deduction
  generally cannot deduct charitable gifts.
- You receive **no goods or services** in exchange for your donation, so the
  **full amount** is generally deductible.
- **Keep a record of every gift.** A bank/Zelle confirmation is sufficient for
  most gifts. For any **single donation of $250 or more**, the IRS requires a
  **written acknowledgment from the organization** — email us to request one.
- Deductions are claimed for the tax year in which the gift is made.
